# Brand Voice — KRYO by Everest Labs

## North Star Brand: Eight Sleep
Mirror their positioning — premium, science-first, outcome-obsessed, aspirational without being arrogant.

## Voice Principles

### 1. Outcome first, feature second
Wrong: 14L reservoir with RV-grade pump
Right: 30 seconds to fully awake. Every morning.

### 2. Science-backed, not woo
Wrong: Feel amazing and refresh your energy
Right: Cold exposure triggers a 250-300% dopamine spike. That is what sharp feels like.

### 3. Short sentences. Punchy. Declarative.
Wrong: KRYO is designed to help you optimise your mornings through the power of cold therapy
Right: Your tap water is 35C. KRYO is 1C. You do the math.

### 4. Premium confidence, never defensive
Wrong: At just $1,000 it is actually quite affordable for what you get
Right: KRYO is $1,000. Your time is worth more.

### 5. Dubai-aware
Reference the problem specifically. Warm tap water. Apartment living. Space constraints.
This is not a generic cold plunge ad. It speaks directly to the Dubai reality.

## Tone by Format

### Ad Copy
- Hook: Bold, specific, curiosity-driving or pain-pointing
- Body: 2-3 punchy lines max, science or ROI, no fluff
- CTA: Direct. Get KRYO. / Wake up differently. / Shop now.

### Landing Page
- Hero: Big outcome claim + one-line proof
- Problem section: Name the Dubai pain specifically
- Solution: Product as the logical answer
- Science: 2-3 credibility points
- Social proof: Testimonials from high-performers
- Offer: Clear, direct CTA

### Instagram/Video
- First 1-3 seconds must hook
- Show the contrast: warm Dubai morning vs ice cold KRYO
- Text overlay: single punchy line per cut

## Words We Use
sharp, awake, cold, performance, morning, ritual, optimise, recover, focus, edge, 1C, 30 seconds, ROI, time

## Words We Avoid
relax, pamper, luxury, affordable, cheap, just, simply, amazing, incredible
