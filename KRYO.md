## The Product — KRYO

### What It Is
KRYO is an apartment-friendly cold shower system. It's a small, plug-in reservoir (camping freezer style) that holds ~14L of water, cooled continuously to around 1°C. An RV-style pump pressurises the water and feeds a standard handheld showerhead mounted to the wall.

When you turn it on, you get 30 seconds of ice-cold water — enough to fully wake you up.

### What It's NOT
- Not a cold plunge (too large, needs space)
- Not just a cold shower (tap water in target markets is 21–35°C — too warm to be effective)
- Not a complicated wellness setup

### The Core Problem It Solves
In cities like Dubai, tap water reaches 35°C+ in summer. You literally cannot take a cold shower. Cold plunges require a balcony or large bathroom. Most city dwellers have neither the water temperature nor the space.

KRYO is the only viable cold therapy option for apartment-dwellers in warm climates.

### Key Product Specs
- Water temp: ~1°C
- Volume: ~14L reservoir (may evolve)
- Setup: Wall-mounted handheld showerhead + plug-in cooling unit + RV pump
- Use time: ~30 seconds per session
- Target retail price: $1,000+ USD

## Brand Positioning

### Brand Model: Eight Sleep
Mirror Eight Sleep's brand closely:
- Premium, science-backed, aspirational
- Sells outcomes not features ("44% more deep sleep" → KRYO: "30 extra minutes of productivity per day")
- Social proof from high-performers
- Sleek, minimal aesthetic
- Talks to people who optimise everything — their sleep, their diet, their time

### Brand Voice
- **Confident, not arrogant**
- **Science-backed claims, not woo**
- **Outcome-first language** (ROI on time, not just "feels good")
- **Premium but not cold** (pun intended)
- Short sentences. Punchy. Direct.

### What KRYO Is Selling
Not a shower. Not a gadget.

KRYO sells **alertness as a competitive advantage.** It sells **time back.** It sells the ability to start the day sharp, without caffeine dependency, in 30 seconds.

## Customer Avatar (Dubai Launch)

### Who They Are
- **Location:** Dubai (warm climate, apartment living)
- **Income:** High — can impulse buy $1,000+ without much deliberation
- **Lifestyle:** Performance-oriented, productivity-focused, health-conscious
- **Living situation:** Apartment — no outdoor space, no room for a plunge pool
- **Prior behaviour:** Morning coffee or espresso to wake up; aware caffeine isn't optimal

### Their Problem
- Can't get cold water from the tap (it's warm year-round)
- No space for a cold plunge
- Wakes up groggy, relies on caffeine
- Loses 30+ minutes each morning to slow starts and caffeine crashes later in the day

### Their Desire
- Be sharp from the moment they're up
- Optimise their morning like they optimise everything else
- Feel in control of their day before it starts
- A high-performance routine that fits their apartment life

### Their Objection (anticipated)
- "Is it worth $1,000?" → Frame ROI: if you value your time at $100/hr, 30 min/day = $18K/year
- "Does it actually work?" → Science + social proof (Joe Rogan, athletes, etc.)
- "Will it fit in my apartment?" → Compact design, standard shower connection

### Key Message Angles to Test (MVP)
1. **Productivity angle** — "Win back 30 minutes every morning"
2. **Caffeine replacement** — "The coffee you don't need"
3. **Dubai problem** — "Dubai tap water hits 35°C. KRYO hits 1°C."
4. **Status/identity** — "Your morning routine, upgraded"
5. **Science angle** — Cold exposure benefits (dopamine, cortisol, alertness)
