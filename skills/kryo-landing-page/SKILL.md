## Landing Page Structure

### Section 1 — Hero
- **Headline**: Bold outcome claim. Max 8 words. No fluff.
- **Subheadline**: Name the problem + tease the solution. 1–2 sentences.
- **CTA button**: Action-first. "Get KRYO" / "Wake Up Differently" / "Shop Now"
- **Trust line**: Under CTA — social proof or guarantee. e.g. "Trusted by 500+ high-performers in Dubai"

### Section 2 — The Problem (Dubai Specific)
- Headline: Name the pain
- 3 bullet points: specific, visceral, relatable
- Must feel like KRYO was built for exactly this person

### Section 3 — How It Works
- 3-step visual explanation (simple, no jargon)
- Step 1: Fill the 14L reservoir
- Step 2: It cools to 1°C automatically, always ready
- Step 3: 30 seconds. Fully awake.

### Section 4 — The Science
- 2–3 science-backed claims with sources or influencer references
- Keep punchy, not academic
- e.g. "Cold exposure triggers a 250–300% dopamine spike (Huberman Lab)"

### Section 5 — Social Proof
- 3 testimonials: high-performer archetype (finance/entrepreneur/athlete)
- Format: Quote → Name → Title/Location
- Focus on: morning transformation, time saved, replaced caffeine

### Section 6 — Product Details
- Key specs in plain English (not tech-speak)
- 14L, 1°C, plug-in, standard shower connection, quiet operation
- Compact dimensions, apartment-friendly

### Section 7 — Offer + CTA
- Price: $1,000 (or current offer)
- What's included
- Guarantee / free shipping / whatever applies
- Final CTA button
- ROI line: "At $1,000, KRYO costs less than the 182 hours you'll win back this year."
